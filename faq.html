How can I tell if a Handshake job posting is legit?
Quick checks before you apply or accept.
>
Legit postings list a real company name, link to a professional website, and use an official company email address (for example @company.com). Job descriptions should be clear, specific, and similar to what you’d see on the company’s own site. If the salary looks way higher than normal or the duties are super vague, slow down and double-check.

Why do scammers target Miami students on Handshake and email?
Why you’re a target, especially during the semester.
>
Students are busy, need experience, and may be looking for quick income. Scammers know that and send fake internship offers, part-time jobs, or “on-campus opportunities” that look like they’re from real Miami offices or professors. Some recent scams have even used real staff names in fake internship emails.

Spotting red flags
Is it normal for an employer to contact me outside of Handshake?
Email, text, and moving off the platform.
>
Yes, but they should still look professional. It’s normal to get follow-up emails about an interview or application, especially from a company domain. Be careful when a “recruiter” immediately moves to personal email, text, or messaging apps and starts asking for personal information or money.

What are the biggest red flags in fake Handshake job offers?
Things that should make you slow down.
>
Watch for:
  • Instant “you’re hired” messages with no interview.
  • Very high pay for very simple work.
  • Requests for bank info, Zelle, or gift cards.
  • Personal email accounts instead of company addresses.
  • Lots of spelling mistakes or copy-paste looking messages.
If a job sounds “effortless” but pays like a full-time role, that’s a bad sign.

Should I ever pay for training, equipment, or software?
When “we’ll reimburse you” is a bad sign.
>
In most student jobs and internships, no. Legit employers don’t ask you to buy gift cards, cash checks, or send money as part of getting hired. If they want you to front money for supplies or “setup fees” and promise to reimburse you later, treat it like a scam until a Miami career advisor helps you verify it.

If something feels off
What should I do if I see a sketchy job on Handshake?
How to report it without overreacting.
>
Don’t apply just to “see what happens.” Instead:
  • Use Handshake’s tools to flag or report the job or employer.
  • Take screenshots or save the text.
  • Ask a Miami career advisor or staff member to look it over.
It’s better to get a second opinion than risk your money or identity.

What if I already clicked a link or shared personal information?
Next steps if things might be serious.
>
Don’t panic, but act quickly:
  • Change any passwords you may have entered.
  • If you shared bank details, contact your bank right away.
  • Forward suspicious messages to InfoSec@MiamiOH.edu.
  • Call IT Help if you think you clicked a malicious link on a Miami account.
The sooner you reach out, the easier it is for Miami staff to help protect your accounts.

3-Second Scam Check
Before responding to any job posting, email, or text, ask yourself:
Too good to be true? No interview? Wants money? Personal email? Weird grammar? “Act now” pressure?
If you say “yes” to any of these, pause. Don’t send money or personal information until you check the job against the resources in this FAQ or talk with a Miami staff member.
